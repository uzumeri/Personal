The center cannot hold. For decades, global trade and innovation relied on massive centralized hubs—Silicon Valley, Wall Street, Shenzhen—because it was the only way to overcome the friction of distance and build liquid markets. 

But that market physics is fundamentally changing.

In my work with AI and market engineering, I’m seeing a massive structural shift happening right before our eyes. The historical tradeoff between market "thickness" and local relevance has been dissolved by artificial intelligence. You no longer have to move your operations or your capital to a Hegemon’s command center just to participate globally. 

This is an existential realization for the "Middle Powers." Right now, countries representing roughly $37 trillion in GDP are deeply frustrated by the confrontational, transactional foreign policies of the US and China. The "American Exceptionalism" doctrine—particularly under MAGA—relies heavily on a massive blind spot: the assumption that the world MUST use American hubs because there is simply nowhere else to go. 

That is no longer true.

Through tools like semantic matching, vector embeddings, and asynchronous AI brokerage, Middle Powers are gaining the ability to wire around the Hegemons. AI can engineer trust and slash search friction across borders, languages, and complex regulations in milliseconds.

We’re already seeing this burst of distributed innovation globally. In my own work, I collaborate with a brilliant team of developers in Addis Ababa. They instantly adopt the newest AI innovations published to the web. The necessity of physical proximity for innovation is dead. 

When I was in Ethiopia recently, I saw local tech communities engineering fintech solutions to solve problems Wall Street wouldn't even conceptualize—like using alternative algorithmic data to collateralize generational farmland that lacks formal deeds. This is happening everywhere, from Toronto to London to Mexico City.

As tech power and AI expertise decentralize, these local communities are going to build high-performance, decentralized networks that preserve their margins, protect their sovereignty, and genuinely surprise Silicon Valley. 

The waning of Hegemon dominance isn't just politics—it's market physics. 🌍
