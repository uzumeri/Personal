For decades, global trade relied on massive, centralized hubs like Silicon Valley and Wall Street. If you wanted to scale, you had to go through them. 

But AI is fundamentally changing the physics of how the world connects.

In my latest Substack piece, I explore a profound structural shift: the rise of the "Middle Powers." Frustrated by confrontational Hegemons like the US and China, these countries are realizing they no longer need to rely on the old hubs.

AI and large language models are dissolving the friction of distance, language, and trust that used to make centralization strictly necessary. Now, we are seeing brilliant, decentralized innovation happening everywhere—from my own dev team in Addis Ababa to the startup networks of Toronto and Mexico City. 

The "America First" movement assumes the world MUST come to the US because there's nowhere else to go. They are about to be very surprised when they find themselves sitting on the sidelines. 🤔
