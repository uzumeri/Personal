Everybody saw the tariffs and the sanctions. What almost nobody sees is what happens next — because it takes months.

Global supply chains have a feedback delay. The shock is visible and dramatic, but the consequences arrive weeks or months later, after the news cycle has moved on. By then, nobody connects the price spike or the factory closure to the decision that caused it.

I wrote about why this delay is the most dangerous feature of the current crisis — and why the people making the decisions are structurally incapable of seeing the damage they're doing.
