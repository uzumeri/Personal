Producing more goods than you can domestically consume isn't "market abuse." It's the literal definition of trade.

Yet that's exactly the justification the new U.S. Trade Representative is using to launch sweeping investigations into 16 major economies.

They are arguing that if a country has "structural excess capacity" in a sector—meaning they produce a massive surplus of a good they don't consume—they are punishing America. It's an argument that contradicts thousands of years of human economic history. 

A farmer who grows more wheat than they can eat isn't defrauding the tractor salesman. The surplus is the very reason exchange exists and why currency was invented. 

When a nation's trade policy criminalizes the core concept of comparative advantage, you have to wonder what's really going on. Are they really this economically clueless? Or is this massive, perplexing trade war just an intentional distraction from other headlines?
