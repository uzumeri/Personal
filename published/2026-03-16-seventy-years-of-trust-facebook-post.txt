The US is asking traditional allies like the UK, Japan, and Australia for military help in the Strait of Hormuz—and the answer, almost universally, is no. 

In any previous decade, they would have answered the call instantly. But Donald Trump has spent the last 12 months systematically destroying America's global trust. The bill for a transactional foreign policy is coming due, and it turns out, no one extends credit when trust is systematically broken. 

I have worked in business for nearly 50 years. Trust is infrastructure. When it breaks, partners don't just leave; they actively route around you. What will come next for a world that has to plan around an unpredictable America?
