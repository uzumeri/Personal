The US spent 70 years building a global alliance network based on trust—and dismantled it in 12 months.

It hasn't taken long for the chickens to come home to roost. Donald Trump is currently asking former allies for help to re-open the Strait of Hormuz, while simultaneously fueling the crisis there. Unsurprisingly, those allies are refusing to help. When you systematically betray trust, people stop extending you credit.

I have worked in business for nearly 50 years. Trust is infrastructure. When it breaks, partners don't just leave; they actively route around you.
